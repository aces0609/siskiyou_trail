# Siskiyou Crest Overland Trail

![Siskiyou Crest](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/5b/Siskiyou_Mountains_view.jpg)

The **Siskiyou Crest Overland Trail** is a remote ridgeline route running along the Oregon–California border through the Siskiyou Mountains. The trail follows high-elevation forest roads with sweeping views of the Klamath Mountains and access to lakes, lookouts, and remote camp areas.

**Total Trail Distance:** ~320 miles  
Route: **Ashland, OR → Briggs Creek Crossing → Exit via Selma, OR**

The trail consists mostly of forest service roads with moderate difficulty sections and occasional rough terrain. Travel speeds are generally slow due to road conditions and scenic stops.

# Suggested 3-Day Itinerary
*(~100 miles per day average)*

Due to road conditions and stops for scenery, photography, and exploration, typical travel speeds on this route average **20–30 mph**.

Estimated daily driving time assumes frequent stops and relaxed overlanding travel.

---

# Day 1  
### Ashland → Applegate Lake  

**Distance:** ~100 miles  
**Estimated Overland Drive Time:** 4–6 hours

This first section climbs quickly into the Siskiyou Mountains and follows scenic ridgeline roads toward **Applegate Lake**.

Highlights:

- Scenic forest roads
- Views of the Applegate Valley
- Multiple developed recreation areas

**Applegate Lake** is a large reservoir with plenty of space for:

- Paddleboarding
- Swimming
- Relaxing shoreline stops

Most camping here is **developed campground sites**, with fewer dispersed options compared to later in the trip.

---

# Day 2  
### Applegate Lake → Bolan Lake  

![Bolan Lake](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/6/61/Rogue-River-Siskiyou_National_Forest.jpg)

**Distance:** ~100 miles  
**Estimated Overland Drive Time:** 5–6 hours

This section continues along the crest through more remote terrain.

Planned route adjustments:

- Use **Forest Road 1040 bypass** to avoid a more technical high-clearance segment.

Fuel Stop:

- **Happy Camp gas station** (~180 miles into the route)

## Bolan Lake Campground

[Bolan Lake Campground Info](https://www.fs.usda.gov/r06/rogue-siskiyou/recreation/bolan-lake-campground)

Facilities:

- Dispersed-style campground
- 2 vault toilets

Costs:

| Type | Cost |
|---|---|
| Single campsite | $10 / night |
| Additional vehicle | $5 per day |

---

## Optional Stay: Bolan Mountain Lookout

[Bolan Mountain Lookout](https://www.fs.usda.gov/r06/rogue-siskiyou/recreation/bolan-mountain-lookout-0)

- Sleeps: 4 people  
- Cost: $65 per night  
- Reservations open: April 1

This restored fire lookout sits above the forest with **360° mountain views**.

---

# Day 3  
### Bolan Lake → Briggs Creek Crossing → Exit via Selma  

**Distance:** ~80 miles  
**Estimated Overland Drive Time:** 4–5 hours

The final section descends from the crest into the Illinois Valley and exits near **Selma, Oregon**.

Along this stretch you’ll find many opportunities for dispersed camping.

Potential camping locations:

- `42°20'59.1"N 123°46'57.2"W`
- `42.374920, -123.767352`

These areas provide good access to forest clearings suitable for multiple vehicles.
